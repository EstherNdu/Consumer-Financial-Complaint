# Consumer-Financial-Complaint

# Brief Introduction
This report delves into customer complaints across 1,081 companies in all 51 U.S. states for Onyx Data Challenge. This report enables in-depth analysis of complaint trends, product and sub-product issues, response timeliness, and company behavior across regions. It demonstrates how large-scale consumer data can be transformed into actionable insights that support regulatory oversight, customer experience evaluation, and strategic decision-making when effectively modeled and visualized. It also highlights Key Performer Indicators such as Total complaints, average timely response, average reputation score and average market share. 

# About The Dataset
This project uses a two-table dataset consisting of 62,516 consumer complaint records and 1,081 company records. The data captures detailed information on financial products, complaint issues, company responses, and resolution timelines. It provides a solid foundation for analyzing complaint patterns, response effectiveness, and company-level behavior.

# Problem Questions
* What is the trend in complaints over the years
* Which channel had the most complaints and how long did it rake to respond via these channels
* Which products had highest complaint share
* Which states had the highest complaint share
* How did complaints vary across states and region
* Which company tier had the highest reputation score
* Do companies with higher market share have high reputation score?

# Tool Used 
  Power Query & Power BI

# Processes 
* Imported the data into Power BI
* Transformed the data using Power Query
* Created a reputation score group column
* Loaded the data into Power BI for Analysis & Reporting
* Modeled the data
* Used DAX to perform indepth analysis
* Created interactive reports

# Key Insights

<img width="1221" height="255" alt="Screenshot 2025-12-26 133247" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c3ee80cf-a4d9-4697-b9b3-fba04d8994b4" />

* There is an increasing upward trend in the number of complaints over the years. In 2019, there was drop from 7,872 to 7,075 complaints. However from 2020-2022 there was steady increase with 2022 recording the highest number of complaints (12,953). In 2023, there was a major drop in complaints (from 12,953 complaints to 9,131)
* Web channel had the highest number of complaints (45,423) followed by referral (10,766) and phone (4,684). Email channel had the least number of complaints(2) and also the highest response time rate (94.67%).

<img width="1094" height="604" alt="Screenshot 2025-12-27 140707" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b0aa653f-595a-4016-a158-dcecda6e7a15" />

* The issues most customers faced centered around managing an account. This issue had over 15,000 complaints followed by incorrect information with 4,931 complaints and purchase statement issue with 4,415 complaints.
* Checking and Savings account product had the highest number of complaints and complaint share with 24,814 complaints and complaint share of 39.69% followed by Credit and Prepaid cards with 16,197 complaints and complaint share of 25.19%

* South Atlantic division had the highest number of complaint followed by Pacific and middle east divisions.
* Four states have almost half of all complaints:- California (21.93%), Florida (10.38%), Texas (7.50%), and New York (7.11%) together account for 47% of complaints. Companies should focus their improvement efforts on these states for the biggest impact.
